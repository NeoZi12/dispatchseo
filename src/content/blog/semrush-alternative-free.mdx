Search "Semrush alternative, free" and the honest answer is never one product - it's the same short list of separate tools every listicle recommends: Google Search Console, Google Keyword Planner, Ahrefs Webmaster Tools, a freemium suite like Mangools or Ubersuggest, and a question-clustering tool like Answer Socrates. Each one replaces a slice of what Semrush does, at no cost, with a real limit attached. What the listicles skip is the part that costs you time once you're running all of them at once: none of the tools know the others exist, so you're the integration layer, copying numbers between six tabs by hand.

> **TL;DR** - There's no single free Semrush replacement, there's a stack: Google Search Console for your own site's data, Google Keyword Planner for keyword ideas (gated behind a Google Ads account), Ahrefs Webmaster Tools for site audit and backlinks (capped at 1,000 results, your own verified sites only), plus a freemium suite (Mangools or Ubersuggest) and a question tool like Answer Socrates. That's seven separate logins doing what one Semrush seat does for $117.33/mo - and none of the seven talk to each other. DispatchSEO is the other model: the same jobs, behind one bearer token and a suggestions queue, run by the Claude Code you already pay for.

## The part every listicle skips: nothing in that stack talks to the others

This is the gap the "top N free alternatives" format can't show, because it's a format built around ranking tools one at a time, not around running them together. In practice: Keyword Planner gives you a volume range, you paste it into a spreadsheet. Ahrefs Webmaster Tools flags a technical issue, you copy it into a different doc. Search Console shows a query climbing, and nothing tells the other six tools that happened - you're the one who has to notice, remember, and act. There's no shared history across a week, no queue of what to do next, nothing that runs on a schedule instead of when you remember to open a tab. That's not a knock on any single tool in the table above; it's the cost of "free" meaning seven separate, unconnected products instead of one system.

## Honest limits: when the stack is still the right call

Nothing here replaces Semrush's actual differentiator - a crawl-scale database covering competitors' sites, not just your own. If the job is "see what a rival ranks for and how they got their backlinks," every free tool above stops at your own verified property, and DispatchSEO doesn't fix that either; `add_backlink_prospect` records prospects a person or an agent found, it doesn't crawl the web the way Semrush or Ahrefs' paid tier does. A wired system also isn't the right trade if you don't already run an agent CLI - building one just to save seven logins is [solving a smaller problem with a bigger one](/blog/self-hosted-ai). The free stack, tab-switching and all, is genuinely the right answer for a single site checked occasionally, or a team that already has a person whose job is doing that manual handoff.

## FAQ

**Is there a completely free alternative to Semrush?**
Not one tool - a stack. Google Search Console and Google Keyword Planner cost nothing and need no card; Ahrefs Webmaster Tools adds a real (if capped) site audit and backlink checker for verified sites. Together they cover most of what a solo site needs; none of them cover competitor research at Semrush's scale.

**What does Semrush actually cost per month right now?**
$117.33/mo for the entry SEO plan, billed annually ($139/mo billed monthly), verified against Semrush's own pricing page. There's no permanent free plan - only a 7-day trial.

**Can Ahrefs Webmaster Tools replace Semrush for backlinks?**
Only for your own sites. It's genuinely free and shows real backlink data, but it's capped at 1,000 backlinks and restricted to verified properties - checking a competitor's backlink profile needs a paid tool.

**Do I need a Google Ads account to use Keyword Planner?**
Yes. Google's own product page confirms Keyword Planner requires a Google Ads account and a created campaign before it opens, even though using it costs nothing.

**Is DispatchSEO free?**
Free in software cost - no per-seat fee - but not free in the way "self-hosted" sometimes implies. It runs on your own Vercel and Supabase accounts, needs the Claude subscription you likely already pay for, and its keyword data is metered per project through whichever research API you connect.

If you already run an agent CLI daily, wiring these jobs together is an afternoon's work, not a new subscription. If you don't, the seven tabs above are still exactly what most solo sites run their SEO on, and there's nothing wrong with that being the whole answer.
