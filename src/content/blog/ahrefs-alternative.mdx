What self-hosting changes is who owns the query layer sitting on top of your own site's data, not the size of the index underneath it.

## When the paid seat is the smarter buy

Stay on a paid tier if the actual job is competitor backlink research at Ahrefs' scale - no self-hosted account, this one included, replicates a crawl index built over a decade of continuous fetching. It's also the wrong trade if you don't already run an agent CLI day to day: standing one up just to dodge a $129/mo bill spends more time than it saves. And if a non-technical teammate needs a dashboard they can drive without touching code, a paid seat delivers that out of the box - a self-hosted backend, by design, hands you the database and the code instead.

## FAQ

**Does Ahrefs have a free tier at all?**
Yes - Ahrefs Webmaster Tools, and it's a real product: site audit and backlink data for sites you've verified. It stops there; a competitor's backlink profile needs a paid tier.

**How much has Ahrefs' entry price moved?**
Lite lists at $129/mo as of this pricing check against ahrefs.com/pricing, with Standard at $249/mo and Advanced at $449/mo. Annual billing knocks up to 17% off any of them.

**Does DispatchSEO charge a subscription instead of Ahrefs'?**
No fee for the software itself, but real costs elsewhere: your own Vercel and Supabase accounts, plus DataForSEO usage billed to your own account and metered to what you query.

**Will a self-hosted backend ever match Ahrefs' backlink index?**
Not by design. It sees what your own connected account can see; Ahrefs' number comes from crawling the open web continuously, at a scale a single project's account never will.

**Does this only make sense if I'm already running Claude Code?**
Essentially, yes - the agent-as-driver model assumes an agent CLI already runs other jobs for you. Standing one up solely to dodge a SaaS seat is a worse trade than just paying for the seat.
